Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the vault and gold holdings

What is the certificate of deposit?

The certificate of deposit is the legal document proving ownership of the gold trunks in storage. It was issued by the security company at the time of deposit and contains all relevant details including the deposit number, description of assets, and terms of storage.

How are demurrage fees calculated?

Demurrage fees are charged by the security company for the ongoing storage and protection of the assets. In this case, the fee is $20 per day, which amounts to $7,300 per year. These fees continue to accrue until the assets are claimed and all outstanding fees are paid.

How was the current value calculated?

The current value is based on the weight and purity of the gold multiplied by the current market price of gold. Gold prices have appreciated approximately 40% over the 5-year storage period, resulting in the increased valuation from $25.8 million to $36.2 million.

What is required to claim the assets?

To claim the assets, the certificate holder must present the original certificate of deposit to the security company and pay all accumulated demurrage fees. Proper identification and any other verification required by the security company's policies must also be provided.
